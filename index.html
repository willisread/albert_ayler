Albert Ayler

The Trials and Tribulations of the Holy Ghost

A Timeline of Albert Ayler's Life

  • 1936 - Born on July 13th, 1936 in Cleveland, Ohio
  • 1951 - Albert joins his first group, Lloyd Pearson and his Counts of Rhythm, at the age of 15
  • 1954 - Graduated John Adams High School
  • 1956 - Left college and joined the army due to a lack of money
  • 1959 - Albert had been playing in the military band which led to his first trip to Europe
  • 1959/1961 - While stationed in Orleans, France, Albert sits in with local jazz musicians in Paris and starts honing his unique playing style
  • 1961 - Discharged from the military and returned to Cleveland
  • 1962 - Returned to Europe and settled down in Sweden
  • 1962 - In the summer of 1962, Albert joins Herbert Katz Quintet and plays a few dates in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1963 - Recorded and released his debut album My Name is Albert Ayler in January of 1963
  • 1963 - Moved back to Cleveland and then on to New York
  • 1964 - Married Arlene Benton
  • 1964 - Danish label Debut organizes a recording session in February for what would become Ayler's album Spirits (aka Witches and Devils)
  • 1964 - Later in 1964 Albert forms his famous trio with bassist Gary Peacock and drummer Sunny Murray and records Spiritual Unity for the famous underground label ESP
  • 1964 - Don Cherry joins the trio and records the soundtrack to the Michael Snow film of the same name New York Eye and Ear Control
  • 1964 - Albert travels with his quartet to Europe and plays dates in Denmark, Sweden and the Netherlands and records anoter album for Debut entitled Ghosts (aka Vibrations)
  • 1964/1965 - Donald Cherry decides to stay in Europe when Albert and group return to New York so Albert's brother, Donald, joins the group on trumpet
  • 1965 - New albums with the new band are released on ESP: the live single sided EP entitled Bells and his final album for ESP, Spirits Rejoice
  • 1966 - The only recording of Albert Ayler and John Coltrane takes place at the Lincoln Center in New York on February 19th, 1966 at the Titans of the Tenor concert
  • 1966 - In November of 1966, Albert travels back to Europe to play in the Newport Jazz Festival in Europe 1966 alongside Sonny Rollins and Max Roach
  • 1966/1967 - Albert is signed to Impulse! Records thanks to John Coltrane persuding Bob Thiele
  • 1967 - John Coltrane dies on July 17th, 1967 and Albert Ayler is one of only two musicians asked to play at the funeral (the other being Ornette Coleman)
  • 1967 - Albert releases his debut album on Impulse!, a concert recording entitled In Greenwich Village followed by the studio recording Love Cry which featured softer remakes of some of Albert's earlier compositions
  • 1968 - Albert's brother Don is pushed out of the group to help continue the mainstreaming of Albert's sound, which can be heard on the album New Grass
  • 1969/1970 - Albert records his last album for Impulse! entitled Music Is the Healing Force of the Universe and sees its release in 1970
  • 1970 - Albert makes his final trip to Europe in July 1970 and plays two concerts at Fondation Maeght and returns to the US to find his contract with Impulse! had been canceled
  • 1970 - On November 25th, 1970 Albert Ayler's body was found floating in the East River, at the foot of Congress Street Pier, in Brooklyn

"Trane was the Father. Pharoah was the Son. I was the Holy Ghost."

-Albert Ayler
